A government site redesign
Department of Justice
The U.S. Department of Justice delivers news to the U.S. public. The goal of the DOJ is to make an accessible & informational site by delivering news & resources to its audience.

date
Jan 2021

Overview
Website ReDesign
For this mobile app redesign my partner and I had three weeks to define a persona, test users & create sketches and wireframes for the mobile app.

The Problem
While testing accesibility, my partner and I found it would be difficult to navigate through the D.O.J. website as a Spanish user.

The Goal
With informational architecture and accesibility in mind, we will create a consistent layout between the Spanish & English D.O.J. Site.

01
Ideation
After looking through the website and discovering many pain points I realized that there was a way to view the information on the D.O.J. website in Spanish. My partner and I began to ideate user flows, brainstorm and find possible solutions for our users.
Key findings
  • 60% of users were not aware they could view the website in Spanish.
  • Users on the website have to scroll to the very bottom of the page to find a translation option.
  • With lack of text hierchy on the homepage and while reading an article, users may get lost and find it difficult to read.
  • Website is not AA Complaiant and requires larger contrast ratio between text and background color.


    • 02
    User Testing
    Testing was done in person by bilingual students in the age range of 20-25. While gathering the results I realized we needed to go back and change our user flow chart because users were very overwhelmed by this website.
    User Flow
    ​Because the navigation bar isn’t translated to spanish along with news page, users find themselves in a cycle between the Spanish and English page without getting to an actual article. ​
    Key findings
  • 20% of users are able to navigate between the Spanish & English news page.
  • 40% of users are able to view the D.O.J. homepage in Spanish.
  • 60% of users were unable to view the same article they viewed in English in Spanish.





    • 03
    HEURISTICTS & ANNOTATIONS
    Based on the results of the four page user path, heuristic evaluations, usability issues and redlining were executed for each page.
    Key findings
  • Translation button is easily overlooked at the bottom of the page.
  • “News” tab doesn’t offer an “articles” option.
  • Inconsistentcy between English and Spanish pages.

    • 04
    wireframes
    Our design team wanted to design a site that is simple to navigate, with minimal distraction. I designed these lo-fi wireframes to focus on assesibility and the news section of this website. I added an option to view the news site collapsed or expanded for long articles that may be difficult to read on a mobile device and allow for a summary to be at the top of the article.


    05
    ​TESTING
    We tested users on recognition of icons, translation options and website recognition. This was a crucial step for us to decide what is comfortable with users on this site. We offered four different ways to translate the site to spanish and gathered data on the most common way to do so.
    Key findings
  • 50% users understood the global icon as a way to change site language.
  • 75% users tested used the navigation bar to translate from English to Spanish.
  • 100% of users are able to navigate between the Spanish & English news page.
  • 100% of users are able to view the D.O.J. homepage in Spanish.
  • 100% of users are able to view the same article in Spanish and English. ​
