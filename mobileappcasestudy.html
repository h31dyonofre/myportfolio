User Experience
HappyCow Case Study
HappyCow is an app for vegan foodies on the go allowing immediate access to restaurants that fit their dietary needs.

Role
UX Researcher

Overview
Mobile App Re-Design
For this mobile app redesign my partner and I had three weeks to define a persona, test users & create sketches and wireframes for the mobile app.

The Problem
While users know which restaurants fit their dietary preference, they do not have easy access to view the menus of those restaurants.

Project Goal
To provide a direct resource to view restaurant menus in-app while offering efficiency to the user creating a less frustrating experience for users trying to decide where to find their next meal.

01
User Research & Testing
We reached out to users already on the app to gather user insights, pain points and goals on the HappyCow app. By creating a google form test for users around the world, my partner and I began to send it out through HappyCow social media groups and within the app chat feature. We narrowed down our results and created a user persona.
Key findings
  • Users on the app travel to different cities frequently.
  • Users typically spend 5 minutes or less looking for a new restaurant or menu.
  • HappyCow is the app of choice to find restaurants that fit dietary needs.
    • “Sherrie, who is a very busy and often scatterbrained student, needs a quick and easy way to find food on-the-go that fits her diet because she doesn’t always have time to cook and vegan takeout can be hard to find.”
    02
    Brainstorm & Ideation
    My parnter and I created a priority matrix which has all of our brainstorming results and analyzes what will have the highest user value while maintaining low efforts to achieve the perfect focus for HappyCow.
    Key findings
  • Including a menu in-app keeps users from bouncing to browsers and the app.
  • Implementing a “Call/Order” button on the app creates an efficient way to complete the users task.
  • Creating drop down options to view more or less information will keep the app minimal and clean.

    • Our user flow below demonstrates the cycle the user will go through with the new app experience curated by user testing results and brainstorming done by my partner and I.
    Key findings
  • Having the option to call, order, or get directions to the restaurant all in one app will help users from bouncing through different apps & become a great resource for vegans/vegetarians on the go.


    • 03
    Prototyping
    We began the wireframing process by creating sketches of our ideal user flow, mainly focusing on easing the order process and creating an all in one app experience.


    The next step was to create our digital wireframes, we used our wireframes to give us a better understanding of the user flow, ease of use and functionality of the app. We used these wirefames to test users.



    We tested users throughout this entire process, we found that including the menu in app is an expectation of users. The feature felt natural to click on and didn’t raise concern because users were familiar with this feature on other apps already.

    04
    What I Learned
  • Desiging a simple layout can help users by removing unwanted or un-related objects from the layout.
  • Creating quick sketches at the beggining of the prototyping process can help generate multiple ideas that can be used to create a/b testing.
  • Testing your prototype is crucial in the design process and showing the progress of the test is helpful for users who tend to over complicate questions or tasks.
