​NON-PROFIT WEBSITE RE-DESIGN
​adopt an ELDER
AnElder.org is a non-profit that pushes for community support towards Navajo Native Elders. During this team project, we analyzed the informational architecture of the website and created a quicker donation process for users on the website.

date
March 2021

Overview
​NON-PROFIT WEBSITE RE-DESIGN
The Problem
​Potential donors don't trust this site with personal card information or understand the key values this non-profit holds due to lack of informational architecture and text hierchy.
The Goal
​ To design an engaging experience between elders & potential donors by giving them easy access to information and donation options via the homepage.

01
Heuristic Analysis
Looking at the homepage on a mobile device, with over 50% of site traffic on mobile devices we can see a huge usability issue right off the bat. The hamburger menu is centered and although it is within the thumb range & easy to navigate to it isn't consistent and involves the Recognition vs Recall heuristic which may lead to a confused user. We were really overwhelmed with lack of informational architecture & user control & freedom because although there is good information on the homepage after scrolling many times the user looses the navigation tab & doesn't have an option to quickly go back to the top of the screen. ​
Key findings
  • Lack of clear hierarchy.
  • Other than the big "Donate" button at the top of the screen, there is no great call to action while reading through the homepage.
  • Doesn't have a way to donate to an specific elder need on the homepage (food runs, flour, firewood, etc)
  • Homepage does not link to a "learn more" or "get involved" under "adopt an elder" section.
  • Mobile version is takes to much screen space above the fold.





    • 02
    User Testing
    For user testing, we used Usability Hub for remote testing & created a 5 second test with follow up questions on site architecture. I sent the test to over 25 people in different age groups that focus on non-profit work.
    Key findings
  • 70% of users tested do not feel comfortable inputting personal information onto this site.
  • 65% of users tested did not understand the mission of the non-profit or their focus. ​


    • 03
    Lo-fi wireframes
    Our design team combined our wireframes to design quick quick and simple design solutions for the redesign. The wireframes you are being presented with are designed in Figma, by me.
    Key findings
  • We found that over 50% of users were using their phones to access our user tests. I realized this was an issue when we only designed for desktop and so I designed my wireframes to be scalable from mobile to desktop.
  • Designing for mobile users is our priority. Adding cards increases website efficiency and doesn't take up the entire screen in the process. Users are also able to view all of their options before needing to scroll all the way to the bottom of the screen.






    • 04
    USER TEST RESULTS
    I designed an a/b test using Maze.co to gather data on design preferences straight from the users. Because we initially designed our own lo-fidelity wireframes for the mobile site, I gathered my teamates designs to find the best possible solution for our final prototype.
    Key findings
  • 67% of users tested prefer a simple navigation system with minimal options for fast desision making.
  • 90% of users tested prefer not to use the term "adopt an elder" or "adopt a native elder."


    • 05
    FINAL PROTOTYPE
    After gathering data and acknowledging many design differences my team and I decided to create our own high fidelity prototypes. I created these using Figma.
    Key findings
  • Build trust between potential donors and "anelder.org." I added the "paypal" payment option to the checkout process to ensure the safety of potential donors.
  • Increasing effiency & consistency to the donation process: Adding cards to the donation page will allow for potential donors to view all of the Native Elders needs in one page without needing to scroll too much.
  • Adding user control & freedom to the design by creating collabsable cards & adding a visibility of system status.
